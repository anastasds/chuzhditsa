The essay

The critic as artist, at machine speed

A language model built a typeface, an orthography, and three academic papers, steered by a man who wrote nothing and refused, on principle, to say how anything should be fixed. The machine wrote this, too.

The project

The system

A designed extension of Cyrillic for writing foreign words so their source pronunciation is recoverable — revived Old Bulgarian letters, imports from the wider Cyrillic commonwealth, and a compositional diacritic grammar, audited across twenty languages.

Paper (PDF) · on LingBuzz

The typeface

Four families from one skeleton set, generated by an ~800-line parametric builder: weight, contrast, terminals, optical corrections, and kerning are code, and the orthography's rules ship as the font's OpenType features.

Paper (PDF) · Download fonts

The process

How it was made: an auditable single-case study of the collaboration — every criticism archived and coded, every count recomputable from the public record by a shipped audit script.
