Dear Editors of Digital Creativity,

I am pleased to submit "The critic and the builder: how the Chuzhditsa papers
were written" for consideration as a research article.

The paper is a documented, auditable single-case study of human-AI production
in a creative-technical task. An AI coding agent constructed a parametric
Cyrillic typeface family — and drafted the manuscripts describing it — steered
by a human who wrote no code and drew no letterforms; his contribution was
criticism, mostly withholding solutions by design. The complete criticism
channel is archived as a machine-readable corpus, and a shipped audit script
re-derives every quantitative claim in the paper from the archived record.
The findings concern what criticism changed (the builder's method more than
its artifacts), where the method broke (solution-free discipline gave way on
a stubborn letterform), where machine verification failed and the human eye
did not, and how criticism itself became partly delegated to machine-run
adversarial panels by the end. I believe this sits squarely in Digital
Creativity's territory: the practical and theoretical dimensions of making
with digital media, reported from inside a new configuration of making.

One aspect deserves plain statement up front. The manuscript was drafted by
the language model whose conduct it documents, under my direction and
editorial control — this is disclosed in the author note, analyzed in the
paper's "Authorship and disclosure" section against ICMJE/COPE and publisher
policies, and declared on the title page per Taylor & Francis's AI policy.
No AI tool is listed as an author; I am accountable for the content. The
AI-mediated production process is the object of study, and the paper's
integrity rests on artifact-level verifiability rather than on testimony:
its numbers are recomputable by any reader from the public record.

The manuscript is approximately 9,400 words. It is not under consideration
elsewhere. Both versions are supplied (complete and anonymized for
double-anonymous review).

Thank you for your consideration.
