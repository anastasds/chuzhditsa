Dear Editors of Written Language & Literacy,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript "Chuzhditsa: a phonological citation
register for Cyrillic" for consideration as a research article.

The paper addresses a layer of writing systems that alphabets fill only
patchily: a conventional, learnable register for writing foreign words so
that their source pronunciation is recoverable. Taking the Japanese katakana
register as the scale proof and the gaps of Slavic Cyrillic loanword
transcription as the problem case, it presents a designed solution — a
computable, inventory-extending citation register built from attested
Cyrillic material — together with a twenty-language coverage evaluation
under an explicit three-way classification (exact, conventionally merged,
declared loss), and a register-marking typeface whose OpenType substitution
and positioning rules are the orthography's rules in executable form. I
believe the combination of orthography design, evaluation methodology, and
the "literacy and technology" dimension makes it a natural fit for the
journal's scope, and the paper engages work published in its pages
(Unseth 2005 on script choice).

The manuscript is approximately 6,700 words (excluding references and a
generated appendix presenting the full audit example set). It is not under
consideration elsewhere. A preprint is posted on LingBuzz (no. 010134), in
line with the publisher's preprint policy; the anonymized manuscript
withholds author identity and the repository reference for double-blind
review, and both can be supplied to the editorial office on request.

All object-language examples are set live in the typeface under discussion,
so the submitted PDF has all fonts embedded; the system's fonts, build
toolchain, and machine-readable audit data are publicly archived under open
licenses.

Thank you for your consideration.
