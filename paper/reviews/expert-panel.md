# Six-expert pre-submission panel on the Chuzhditsa paper

Six independent domain reviews, each reading the manuscript cold and verifying
claims in its own field. Verdicts ranged from "accept with reframing" to "major
revision"; none rejected the artifact. Condensed below; full reasoning was
captured at review time. Findings are triaged in `expert-panel-triage.md`.

## Phonologist — verdict: clever, but coverage claims overstated
Strengths: the Indic laryngeal×place stress test is correct throughout; the
laryngeal gradient and French nasal system are well-motivated. Errors found:
1. **Arabic emphatics /tˤ dˤ sˤ ðˤ/ merged to plain, undeclared** (Qaṭar→Қатар);
   the Arabic row shows no loss. Major undeclared merger.
2. **/ʔ/** claimed "written in Arabic" but no grapheme exists in Table 2 —
   self-contradiction in the merger note.
3. **Open/close mid vowels /e–ɛ/, /o–ɔ/, /ø–œ/** merged (only е/о, and ӧ=/ø œ/),
   yet Italian, French, Portuguese, Bengali audited as no-merger. "Exact" is lossy.
4. **Vietnamese phonemic vowel length neutralized under tone** (length always
   drops when a tone is present) — undeclared segmental loss.
5. **Mandarin neutral tone collides with Tone 1** (both unmarked) — undeclared.
6. **Persian Qom/dugh** splits the Tehran /q/–/ɢ/ merger by Arabic spelling,
   contradicting the declared merger and Principle 1 (pronunciation, not spelling).
7. **"Retraction" hook mislabels с→ҫ, з→ҙ**: interdental is *advancement*, the
   opposite direction; the operation isn't directionally coherent.

## Slavicist — verdict: historically literate; two hard errors
Strengths: yus values correct (ѫ/ɔ̃/, ѧ/ɛ̃/, ѩ/ѭ); the Polish ą/ę = "yuses meeting
their descendants" point is exactly right and philologically earns credibility;
Preslav/Greek-uncial/Cubberley handling accurate; ѕ credited to Macedonian
without chauvinism. Errors found:
1. **"Last missal printed in Glagolitic, 1905"** — the last *wholly* Glagolitic
   missal is 1905 (Parčić); the last *printed* Glagolitic missal is Vajs 1927.
2. **ѫ "in use until 1945" conflated with the /ɔ̃/ nasal value** — the pre-1945 ѫ
   was non-nasal /ɤ~ə/; nasality was lost ~11th c. Two eras presented as one.
3. **Abstract classes ѧ as "historical Bulgarian orthography"** — ѧ was absent
   from modern Bulgarian orthography (Table 2's "Old Bulgarian" is accurate).
Minor: ѕ is *also* a revival (obsolete Cyrillic-wide, readopted 1944), not purely
a living import — worth stating for consistency with the yus framing.

## Typologist — verdict: artifact stands, taxonomy overstated
1. **"Mostly unoccupied middle layer" overstated** — dictionary pronunciation
   respelling, furigana/ruby, and Arabic/Hebrew vocalization already occupy it.
   Novelty = extensible inventory + typeface register, not an empty joint.
2. **"Featural in Sampson's sense" is the loosest claim** — Sampson's featurality
   is shape-iconicity (Hangul); consistent diacritics ≠ featural (cf. Czech,
   Vietnamese, IPA). Only the laryngeal gradient reaches iconicity. Retire the
   umbrella claim; keep "compositional diacritic grammar."
3. **Coverage metric near-unfalsifiable** — author defines the mergers, so no
   phoneme can fail; it measures conformance to the author's map. Add an external
   criterion (functional load, minimal-pair recoverability, blind convergence) or
   rename it a "representational-capacity audit."
4. **Sample bias**: complex tone (only Mandarin/Vietnamese), ATR/vowel harmony,
   phonation, ejectives/implosives are absent or routed to loss — the sample omits
   what the system handles worst, inflating the exact-rate.
5. English vowel-length marking (ӣ "weekend") imports a contrast GA lacks.

## Graphic designer — verdict: elegant, but the load-bearing visual claim is fragile
1. **Register-by-font premise is structurally overloaded** — a monoline
   round-bowled Cyrillic reads *modern-geometric* OR *ancient-liturgical*, neither
   "foreign"; the specimen words read as a decorative display face — the exact
   "merely a display font" failure §11 concedes.
2. **"Exactly as katakana" (principle 6) contradicts §11** — katakana is an
   *encoding* contrast (plain-text stable); this is a *rendering* contrast that
   dies under font fallback. Soften.
3. **The critical figure is missing** — no image of Chuzhditsa words set inline in
   a body sentence at reading size against a neutral face; that single image *is*
   the register claim. Add it; ideally A/B against italics and ruby.
4. Below-baseline marks (hook, yus tail) collide with descenders; show 8–24pt.
5. unicameral (§2.3) vs two-case (§8) terminological slip.
6. Add an accessibility note: a purely visual register is invisible to screen
   readers and to the plain-text fallback.

## Conlang/conscript — verdict: strong engineering, under-read in the literature
Missing prior art (should cite):
1. **Interslavic** — a living zonal Slavic conlang whose extended Cyrillic already
   revives ѣ ѧ ѫ; the closest living relative to the superset move, entirely absent.
2. **Dictionary pronunciation respelling** — the existing occupant of the citation
   layer; citing it *strengthens* the case (proves demand).
3. **Featural-conscript lineage** for the gradient heuristic: Bell's *Visible
   Speech* (1867), Shavian/Quikscript, Franklin's phonetic alphabet, Unifon.
4. **Initial Teaching Alphabet + spelling reform** as adoption controls — they
   isolate the transition-cost / dual-system risk better than Esperanto/Deseret,
   and ITA collapsed on exactly the "leave-the-register" burden Chuzhditsa incurs.
Self-placement ("with Esperanto and Deseret, not katakana") is right; the hook/ɕ
compromises read as principled, well-declared, not fatal.

## Eastern European studies — verdict: self-aware, but under-reads Cyrillic's politics
1. **Cyrillic-as-identity nearly absent** — no Serbian digraphia, Kazakh/Moldovan
   Latinization; proposing to *add letters to Cyrillic* lands in that charged space.
2. **ѕ attribution asymmetry** — yuses claimed as "Bulgarian revival," ѕ as
   "Macedonian," though both are OCS/Bulgaro-Macedonian heritage; ѕ is a front line
   of the Bulgaria–N. Macedonia dispute. Add one sentence on its contested status.
3. **"Pan-Slavic superset" is not politically innocent post-2022** — carries
   19th-c. pan-Slavist and Russian-hegemony freight; "superset" quietly makes
   Russian the unmarked large inventory. Disclaim pan-Slavism; prefer a neutral term.
4. **Latent Bulgarian primacy** (Preslav, "primary locus") — move the "records the
   pilot, not a hierarchy" disavowal earlier and apply it to the heritage claims.
Facts check out (1945 reform, ѕ 1944 revival, Vuk/Mrkalj, Glagolitic chronology).
