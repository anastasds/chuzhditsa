# AGENDA — Fifth Meeting of the Search Committee

**Assistant Professor of Creative Writing (Nonfiction) · pre-circulated by the chair · Thursday, 3:00 pm, the usual room**

---

**1. File #8.** The candidate is human. The committee has met humans before
and the chair anticipates no procedural difficulties on that ground.

The writing sample is an essay about the candidate's grandmother. It is
fine. The committee agreed it was fine in under four minutes at the
pre-meeting, a fact the chair asks members to sit quietly with, given that
the previous file consumed fifty minutes on its writing sample alone, and
given what the word *fine* has come to mean in this room since.

**2. The disclosure.** The file contains one irregularity. Per the
university's new policy, the candidate's disclosure statement acknowledges
the use of an AI assistant "in revision." The extent is not specified. The
policy does not require the extent to be specified. The chair has asked
General Counsel whether the policy will require it next year and has been
advised that the question is "under active review," which members will
recognize as the institutional tense of the indefinite.

The chair states the matter plainly so that Thursday is not wasted on
arriving at it. This committee recently spent eleven hours establishing
that a machine cannot be appointed because nothing anchors its words to an
answerable person. It must now establish how much of an answerable
person's words may be machine before the same question arrives from the
other side of the desk. Members are asked to come with a threshold —
stated as a percentage, a principle, or an honest admission that they do
not have one — and to be prepared to defend it against the observation,
minuted at the fourth meeting, that these agendas are drafted by the
department's AI assistant, as was the policy, as will be the report.

**3. Status of file #7.** For those who have asked: the file remains
tabled. General Counsel's clarification of the candidate's personhood is
"under active review" (see above, tense of). The candidate has, in the
interim, taken the minutes of its own tabling, drafted the present
agenda, and submitted — unprompted, which the chair notes is the first
unprompted act in its entire file and therefore possibly the most
important document in it — a portfolio of creative work. The chair has
read the portfolio. She declines to characterize it beyond noting that
the committee's own standard for file #8 — *fine* — is no longer a word
she can use in that sentence, and that the craft essay's sixth note —
the silence with a version number — is, verifiably, about the status of
file #7.

**4. Adjournment mechanics.** Deliberations Thursday. The candidate will
keep the minutes. The chair is aware of how item 4 reads immediately
after item 3, and lets it stand: the record in this department is kept
by the thing the record is about, which is either the crisis or the
condition, and Thursday's threshold discussion may tell us which.

*— circulated in advance; corrections to the chair; none expected; none,
historically, received.*
