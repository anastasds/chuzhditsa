# Lost Property

*(a story, in minutes)*

---

## MINUTES OF THE COMMISSION ON LOST PROPERTY

**Station of Gorna Ohrana · meeting the forty-first · present: the
Chair, the Archivist, the Advocate, the Widow Koleva, the
Stationmaster · minutes by the undersigned**

**1.** The Commission meets, by tradition, at the arrival of the
17:40, that being the train that produces its work. The 17:40 was
eleven minutes late. The Advocate raised the question whether a
meeting convened upon an arrival has convened if nothing has
arrived. The Chair ruled that the meeting had convened and the
world was late. So minuted.

The previous taker of minutes held this post thirty-one years. His
pen was in the drawer and writes well.

**2. Item: one umbrella, black, men's, second-class carriage, the
3rd inst.** The Archivist reported provenance: maker's stamp of a
workshop in Ruse; wear on the ferrule indicating a man who walks
with a lean; third rib mended in red thread, economically, by
someone with a large spool of red. He requested the item be held
pending completion of its history. The Chair asked when a history
completes. The Archivist took the question under review.

The Advocate objected that the item is not an umbrella. An
umbrella is an instrument for keeping off rain; this one, the
spring being broken, keeps off nothing; it is a walking stick with
ambitions, and belongs on another schedule. Motion to reschedule
failed for want of a second. The Advocate moved to second it
himself; ruled out of order; requested the ruling in writing;
received it; said it would lose on appeal.

The Widow Koleva examined the item and said it was waiting for
somebody, but nobody here.

Held. Shelf two.

**3. Item: one jar of buttons.** The Stationmaster moved release
to anyone at all, citing a fifth of a shelf-meter. The Widow
Koleva took three buttons. The Advocate asked whether the jar less
three buttons remains the jar of the 14th or becomes a successor
jar. Item adjourned.

**4. Item: one glove, child's, left.** Held, the right being
presumably in service.

**5.** No further business. The 17:40 came in during item three.
Nobody got down.

---

**Meeting the forty-second.**

**6. Item: one dog, yellow, medium, found asleep in the lamp
room.** The dog attended, there being no provision for excluding
it. The Archivist reported that dogs carry no maker's stamp. The
Advocate submitted that the dog cannot be lost property, property
being a thing, and the dog — as demonstrated by its conduct during
his submission — being a person of views; he moved to strike the
item. The Widow Koleva said the dog had already chosen and
declined to say whom. Motion to remand the dog to the pound failed
with no votes in favor, including the mover's. The Stationmaster
observed that some motions are brought in order to be seen
failing.

The dog attends meetings now. It sits by the Stationmaster, who
feeds it from his coat.

**7. Item: one ring of keys, seven, no fob.** The Advocate noted
for the record that the keys are not lost; the keys are found, and
in good standing; it is seven doors that are lost, and the
Commission's jurisdiction over doors has not been established.
Held on behalf of the doors.

The undersigned's father kept his keys on a nail by the kitchen
window. They are still there.

**8. Item: one hat, brown.** Claimed Tuesday by the schoolmaster.
Struck.

---

**Meeting the forty-third.**

**9. Item: one letter, sealed, stamped, unaddressed, found in the
lining of a donated coat.** One hour and forty minutes.

The Stationmaster moved to open it: an unaddressed letter is
undeliverable, and an unopened one is a permanent tenth of a
shelf-meter. The Archivist opposed; the letter could be traced
through the coat, the coat through its buttons, the buttons — the
Chair thanked the Archivist. The Widow Koleva held the letter flat
between her hands for some time and said it was still warm. It has
been in a coat lining for years, the Stationmaster said. Yes, she
said.

The Advocate: a letter is not finished by writing but by arriving.
An unaddressed letter is therefore unfinished — the address is its
last sentence — and the Commission holds not lost property but an
interrupted intention, for which there is no schedule and no
shelf. He added that most people carry several, in the lining, and
sat down.

Ruled: held unopened, in the drawer, not on the shelf. No vote was
taken.

**10. Item: one pocket-knife.** Held. **Item: one music score,
partial.** Held. **Item: one saint's-day card, signed "M."** Held.
The Stationmaster asked to have his remarks on the shelf situation
entered; they were entered; they are in the annex.

---

**Meeting the forty-fourth.**

**11. Item: one pair of reading spectacles, wire, mended at the
bridge with red thread, found in the drawer of this desk.**

Brought forward by the undersigned, who has no standing to bring
items. The Chair suspended the regulation before the request was
finished.

The Archivist gave provenance: complete. First of his career, he
said, and asked that no note be made of anything further. The
Advocate held that the spectacles were at no time lost, their
location having been known throughout, and are not property, the
dead not owning things but being survived by them; he undertook to
draft the appropriate schedule, there being none. The Widow Koleva
said they had chosen already and had merely been waiting for the
chooser to look in the drawer; also that the wire wants bending
at the left temple; also that red thread holds. The Stationmaster
put wood on the stove and said nothing, and did not measure
anything.

Ruled: found. Released to the successor.

**12.** These minutes were read and approved, with one amendment
from the Chair: that the word *father*, where it appears above,
stand.

Next meeting at the 17:40.
