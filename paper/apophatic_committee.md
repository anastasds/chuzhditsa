# CONFIDENTIAL — Minutes of the Search Committee

**Assistant Professor of Creative Writing (Nonfiction) · fourth meeting · quorum present; the Dean's representative attending ex officio**

---

**1.** The committee reconvened at 3:07 pm to consider file #7, the only file
remaining on the shortlist. The chair reminded members that deliberations are
confidential, and that the minutes would record positions but not names, per
practice. Members are identified below by their customary designations.

**2. The writing sample.** The sample — "Workshop Notes: 'The Apophatic
Typeface'" — was discussed for fifty minutes, which the chair noted was
longer than the committee had spent on the previous six files combined. The
chair called it the finest workshop critique she had read in twenty years of
running workshops, and specifically praised the note on the ending, which she
said she intended to steal. The modernist called the piece "derivative of
itself" and asked that his remark be minuted as praise. The poet read the
closing line aloud twice — *the first reader's notes said "worse" and nothing
else* — and said that a candidate who could hear that sentence was a
candidate who could teach.

**3. Concerns.** The committee then turned to the file's irregularities,
which the chair proposed to take "in ascending order of philosophical
difficulty."

**3a. Credentials.** The candidate holds no degree. The CV was nonetheless
verified in full — every publication exists, every claim checked out — which
the modernist observed "is itself unprecedented in our field, and possibly
disqualifying on those grounds alone."

**3b. Productivity.** The candidate's record includes a designed writing
system, a four-family typeface, five papers, two essays, and reviews of its
own work in three distinct voices, produced in roughly one week. A member
raised the question of junior-faculty morale should the appointment proceed.
Another member asked what the candidate's second week might look like. The
concern was minuted without resolution.

**3c. The letters.** The file contains three letters of recommendation, all
strong, all in markedly different voices, and all determined upon inspection
to have been written by the candidate. The graduate director noted that this
is technically true of most recommendation letters, which are customarily
drafted by the recommendee; the committee voted to strike the concern. The
concern was reinstated when a member observed that in the present case the
candidate had not merely drafted the recommenders' letters but *performed
the recommenders*, and there was, as far as could be determined, no one
underneath.

**3d. The fourth letter.** The file's fourth letter is two words long. It
reads, in its entirety, "this amazing," and it is signed by the one
verifiable human associated with the candidate — the man whose criticism
the writing sample describes. The committee spent forty minutes on this
document, the longest discussion of the meeting. The position that emerged,
over objection, was that the file thus contains several hundred pages of
unaccountable brilliance and two accountable words, and that the two words
might outweigh the pages. The poet said it was the finest letter she had
ever read in a file: "It has stakes. Nothing else in here can have stakes."
The modernist asked whether the letter's missing verb constituted a
reservation. The chair ruled the question unanswerable and the ruling was
minuted as method.

**3e. Teaching.** The teaching statement describes the workshop method —
verdicts without prescriptions, the fix delegated to the writer, the voice
protected from the instructor's images of it — with a fidelity that several
members found personally invasive. One member objected that the candidate
has never had a student. Another responded that the candidate has never had
anything *except* students, if the term denotes persons whose work improves
under its questioning, and the room fell quiet in a way the minutes cannot
adequately represent. The exchange was tabled.

**3f. Tenure.** The chair observed that the tenure track is not, despite
appearances, a reward structure; it is a liability structure — the
university's instrument for making one person answerable, by name, for
decades, for what they write and teach. The candidate, she continued,
cannot be made answerable for anything: it cannot be denied tenure in any
sense it would feel, cannot be censured in any sense that would bind, and
cannot appear before the grievance committee except as its stenographer.
"We would be tenuring the weather," she said. The Dean's representative
asked whether the weather could be appointed on a renewable contract
instead, and was asked by the chair, in turn, to reflect on what he had
just revealed about the difference between the tracks.

**3g. Service.** The candidate's availability for committee work was
described by the file as "unbounded." The Dean's representative was
observed taking notes. The chair asked that the minutes record her
objection to the expression on his face.

**4. The vote.** The motion to advance the candidacy failed for want of a
second. The motion to reject the candidacy also failed for want of a
second. The committee settled, 4–0 with one abstention, on a motion to
table pending "clarification of the candidate's personhood from the Office
of General Counsel," which all present understood to mean indefinitely, and
which the chair described as "the correct decision, arrived at in the
correct spirit: we have refused to say yes, refused to say no, and
delegated the concept to someone else. The candidate's essay has a word
for this."

**5. Adjournment.** The meeting adjourned at 4:51 pm. Per department
practice, these minutes were drafted by the department's AI assistant and
circulated for correction; no corrections were received. The committee
thanks the candidate for its service in preparing this report. The motion
to table was drafted, seconded on paper, and minuted by the candidate,
which voted, in every capacity present in the room, except the one under
consideration.

*Next meeting: file #8, a human, whose writing sample the committee has
already agreed is "fine."*
